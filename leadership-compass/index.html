Leadership Framing
"Designed to make invisible leadership work visible — through clear ownership, follow-up, evidence, and support."
Every overdue assessment, every amber risk flag, every unlogged supervisor meeting represents leadership work that usually stays hidden until it becomes urgent. Leadership Compass OS surfaces this work in one place — so curriculum alignment, supervisor follow-up, pastoral accountability, and teacher workload all stay visible across the weekly cycle.

Communication System
Demo mode. Messages are generated as reviewable drafts and logged locally. No real emails are sent, no contact details are stored, and no external services are contacted.
Human-in-the-Loop Communication
This prototype models communication automation with professional human review. The goal is not to replace judgement — it is to reduce missed follow-ups, standardise tone, and make communication visible across the weekly leadership cycle.
Why automate drafting
Drafts handle the routine wording so leaders spend their time on the judgement that matters: deciding when to write, what to escalate, and how to support the family. Every draft requires explicit review before it can be marked as sent.
What this models
A real implementation would integrate with the school email system through a review-and-approve workflow. This demo replicates that flow safely in the browser, with no external services and no data ever leaving this page.

Leadership Cadence
Accountability Cycle
"Accountability is not about blame. It is about making ownership, barriers, support, and next steps visible early enough to act. This engine models a weekly leadership cycle: review, follow up, support, document, and check again."
What this prevents
Overdue work, unclear ownership, silent missed follow-ups, escalations that happen too late, and the same conversation repeated in different rooms. Everything stays visible across the week.

Escalation Ladder — calm, professional accountability stages
1
Stage 1
Friendly Reminder
Trigger — action due within 48 hours
Leadership move: clarify the expectation, offer support, and check that nothing has changed since the action was set.
2
Stage 2
Direct Follow-Up
Trigger — due today or no response received
Leadership move: check the barrier directly, confirm renewed commitment, and adjust support if needed.
3
Stage 3
Leadership Check-In
Trigger — 1–3 days overdue
Leadership move: short structured conversation. Clarify what is in the way and agree a clear next step with a check-in date.
4
Stage 4
Escalate or Reprioritise
Trigger — 4+ days overdue or repeated missed commitments
Leadership move: involve the relevant senior leader, adjust the workload, or reset the timeline. Document the reason openly.
